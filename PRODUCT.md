# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

N°55 Laboratorium serves design-conscious, ingredient-aware skincare customers aged 30–55 with sensitive, reactive, dehydrated, or early ageing skin. They want effective care without unnecessarily aggressive actives, inflated claims, or an overcomplicated routine.

Visitors should quickly understand the brand story, learn how product-specific specifications and barrier-conscious formulation shape the routine, explore the products in order, compare the three serum choices, and understand the claim logic behind every formula. The primary actions are Explore the Routine, View Products, Learn the Science, and Join the Launch List.

For sensitised or compromised skin, the recommended entry point is a simple cleanser and moisturiser pairing. Customers can add toner, a chosen serum, sunscreen, and mask only after skin condition and tolerance improve.

## Product Purpose

N°55 Laboratorium is a Copenhagen-inspired skincare system formulated in Australia for skin equilibrium. Its fragrance-free formulas support sensitive and age-supportive routines through product-specific specifications, barrier-conscious formulation, and clear evidence-led education. Copenhagen is the design influence, not a claim of Danish manufacture; pH is one formula parameter where relevant, not a universal benefit claim.

The core routine is Cleanser, Toner, Serum, Moisturiser, Sunscreen, and Mask. Rav Perle, Glød, and PinPerle are alternative choices within the serum step: Rav Perle for brightening and antioxidant support, Glød for repair-support and soothing comfort, and PinPerle for plumping hydration and age-supportive care.

The site sells the world and point of view of N°55 first, then supports product discovery, routine education, trust, and conversion. Success means visitors understand the system without feeling overwhelmed and feel confident choosing a safe starting routine.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and cultivated. The voice is premium, evidence-led, barrier-conscious, and quietly authoritative. It explains formulas in plain language, avoids fear-based selling, and treats restraint as a form of care.

Reference qualities include Aesop's architectural calm and maturity, Le Labo's formula metadata and prepared-with-care cues, Typology's ingredient clarity, Byredo's product-as-object confidence, and the science-backed positioning of Augustinus Bader and Dr. Barbara Sturm. N°55 should remain warmer, more accessible, and more specific than these references.

## Anti-references

Avoid sterile dermatology-clinic aesthetics, cold medical blue, stock model imagery, and hospital-like interfaces. Avoid generic clean-beauty imagery such as leaves, water splashes, beige bottles, and vague sustainability claims. Avoid loud pastel beauty styling, cute icons, TikTok gradients, glow effects, excessive motion, and inflated claims.

Do not use black-and-gold luxury clichés, ornate perfume-counter decoration, direct Aesop or Le Labo imitation, dense apothecary labels, low-contrast beige-on-beige styling, or a purely functional ecommerce template with no brand world-building. The experience must not become cold, celebrity-led, fragrance-oriented, emotionally vague, or expensive-looking without substance.

## Design Principles

1. Make the system legible before making it expansive. Explain barrier care, product-specific formulation and routine order before presenting deeper formula detail.
2. Earn trust through specificity. Use accurate claims, product-specific ingredient status, formula numbers, structured metadata, and restrained language instead of prestige signals or hype.
3. Protect the starting customer. Lead reactive or compromised skin toward cleanser and moisturiser first, then make progression through the routine deliberate and easy to understand.
4. Let products behave like considered objects. Use pacing, photography, typography, and negative space to create confidence without copying an apothecary label system.
5. Keep refinement usable. Every interaction, label, and visual distinction must remain readable, accessible, and robust across devices.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain strong text and control contrast, visible keyboard focus states, semantic HTML, logical heading order, useful alternative text, skip links, accessible forms, and comfortable mobile touch targets.

Do not rely on color alone for SKU or serum differentiation. Pair Morandi accents with product names, formula numbers, labels, and layout cues. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by softening or removing nonessential animation. Accessibility must be treated as part of the premium experience, never as a visual compromise.
